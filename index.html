R1
IIR Lab · RUC Sep 2025—Present

Risk-aware multi-agent systems under real-world perturbations

Manuscript in preparation for AAAI

I formalize long-tailed execution cost in multi-agent systems as a tail-risk problem: a small number of poor operator-routing decisions can cause disproportionately expensive failures. The work augments PPO with a Conditional Value at Risk objective and an adaptive Lagrangian multiplier to prioritize rare, high-cost errors.

At inference time, a quantile-regression cost predictor collaborates with a centralized topology controller through logit bias. The system is evaluated across six realistic perturbation families—including input noise, reasoning hallucination, context truncation, and operator loss—on benchmarks such as MMLU-Pro.

  • CVaR
  • PPO
  • Cost prediction
  • Robust evaluation
R2
Qiushi Academic Project · National level Mar 2025—Mar 2026

Semantic medical image retrieval

I developed a dual-route semantic embedding framework for medical image retrieval. The knowledge-enhanced route expands entities from radiology reports with UMLS, while the content-aware route aligns ViT image features with BioClinicalBERT text representations through multimodal contrastive learning.

On MIMIC-CXR, the fused retrieval index improved mAP@10 by approximately 12% over a ResNet-feature baseline and reached 71.3% Recall@10.

  • MIMIC-CXR
  • UMLS
  • ViT
  • BioClinicalBERT
